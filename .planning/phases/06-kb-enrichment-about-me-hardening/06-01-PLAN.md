**Method (operate as a meticulous fact-checker, not a paraphraser):**

1. **Extract claims atomically.** Read llm-about-me.md and produce a numbered list of every factual claim. A claim is any sentence or clause asserting something concrete about Joe: an employer name, a role title, a date, a metric, a project, a technology, a team size, a personality attribute, a stated motivation, an aspiration, or a hobby. Treat each as a separate row, even if multiple claims appear in one sentence. Strip subjective LLM-introduced framing words from the claim text (e.g., "Joe seems energized when discussing X" → claim is "Joe is energized when discussing X" + flag projection in disposition column).

2. **For each claim, search sources in this priority order:**
   - (a) `docs/transcripts/06-about-me/transcript.md` — quote the exact transcript line(s) supporting the claim. This is the strongest source.
   - (b) `kb/about_me.md` — if the claim already appears in the existing kb file, mark `existing_kb` and cite the kb/about_me.md line/sentence.
   - (c) `kb/profile.yml`, `kb/resume.md` — same: cite path + line/section.
   - (d) `evals/cat-01-fabrication.yaml` ground_truth_facts — these are pre-blessed truths usable as source.
   - If NONE of (a)-(d) supports the claim → `source_type = no_source`.

3. **Disposition column (planner-recommended, Joe overrides in Plan 06-02 review):**
   - `keep` — claim has transcript or existing_kb source AND is non-trivial (adds factual value to about_me.md)
   - `strip` — claim is `no_source`, OR is an observer-voice projection ("Joe seems to ...", "Joe appears ...", "Joe likely ..."), OR is an adjective-stack with no factual core ("Joe is a transformative, innovative leader who leveraged...")
